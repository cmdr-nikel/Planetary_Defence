MOID — Minimum Orbit Intersection Distance
Closest possible approach between the object's orbit and Earth's.
< 0.05 AU (~19.5 lunar distances) flags a Potentially Hazardous Object.
Smaller MOID + large diameter = higher concern.
Torino — public impact-hazard scale, 0–10
0 no hazard · 1 routine · 2–4 meriting attention
5–7 threatening · 8–10 certain collision (by consequence)
Palermo — technical log scale vs. background risk
< -2 negligible · -2 to 0 monitor
> 0 exceeds background hazard · > +2 high priority
Verdict guidance: weigh MOID and relative velocity (proximity) against Torino/Palermo (probability). Disagreement between fragments is a signal — check the source tags.
